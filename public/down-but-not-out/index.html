Down But Not Out

About 4 months ago, the entire team that I was working with was let go. I lost a job that day, but I was full of hope. The team was trying to find a place where most if not all of us could hop over to and continue working together. After a few weeks of looking, we decided that we needed to go our seperate ways. There aren’t many companies that would be willing to hire a team of people without them bringing a product with them.

I quickly found out that many companies would be interested in me, even as a junior programmer. I got 3 offers, and took the startup option, as I thought that I could have a larger impact at the startup than at other jobs, and I also could work remotely, allowing me to not have to deal with the horrible traffic in the DC area. After a few months, however, it became clear that the environment at this company was not going to work for me. I needed to sit next to a senior developer, not because I needed to be babied, but because I could quickly resolve issues that I had with their codebase and build tools. When the team met in person, I was 10x more productive then when I was working on my own.

So I left the job. I’ve been searching for the right opportunity, where people factor in letting me learn on the job when they hire me. I only have one year of experience, but I think, if you look at what I accomplish in a good environment, that I would be a good investment to any company.

But anyways, this post wasn’t supposed to turn into an advertisement. Instead, it was to try and frame what this blog will be for the time being. While I work on finding a new job, I will try and write down the different side projects that I am working on, and anything that I have learned while trying to find a job. Hopefully it will be helpful to someone who is in a similar circumstance, one day.
